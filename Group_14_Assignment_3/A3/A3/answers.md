### Answers to the questions

#### 1. In which year and in which weather station has the lowest temperature been recorded?
- The lowest temperature was recorded in 2012 at the Feldberg weather station (specifically hitting -19.7°C).

#### 2. Are there time-based patterns regarding humidity?
- Yes, there is a seasonal/cyclical pattern where humidity is highest during the winter months and lower during the spring and summer months.

#### 3. Is there some kind of relation between temperature and humidity?
- Yes, there is a negative relationship between temperature and humidity. As temperature rises, humidity generally tends to drop. As can be seen in the Parallel Coordinates plot, the lines traveling between the Temperature axis and the Humidity axis frequently cross each other.

#### 4. a) Is air pressure lower by the sea or at continental locations? 
- Air pressure is generally higher by the sea. In the Parallel Sets plot it can be seen that the flow of ribbons starting from the "Coastal" node flows almost entirely into the "High" pressure category on the final axis. In contrast, a much larger portion of the "Continental" flow ends up in the "Low" pressure category.

#### 4. b) Is there a difference in air pressure between sea-level stations and mountain-based stations? 
- Yes, there is a difference. Mountain-based stations record lower air pressure than sea-level stations. This is visible in the Parallel Sets plot. By hovering over the "Mountain" node on the Elevation axis, the highlighted ribbon flows almost exclusively down into the "Low" pressure node.
