Introduction

In this assignment, you will apply your knowledge about multi-attribute data visualizations and practice implementing interaction techniques (manipulation, faceting, reducing) to create an interactive coordinated multi-view.

To give you some freedom in your design and allow you to apply your visualization knowledge from the lectures, the task will only define affordances. You will yourself decide which way to apply them. Please, be able to defend your choices in visualization terms.

Task

Design and implement an interactive, coordinated multi-view about the winter seasons in the Olympic athletes dataset in "data/athlete_events.csv". The interactive visualizations should work towards answering the following questions:

  1. Does an athlete need to have certain bodily attributes (like height and weight) to be successful in certain sports?
  2. Are there sports that are dominated by certain teams?
  3. Are certain sports more popular for men or women in certain teams?

Hints: Success can be measured by the number of medals won. Beware, that the number of medals that could be won is not the same for all sports.

Affordances

The interactive, coordinated multi-view should have the following features:

  1. At least one heatmap and one scatter plot (30%).
  2. At least three interaction techniques (50%):
    1. At least one manipulation technique
    2. At least one faceting technique
    3. At least one reducing technique

You are not allowed to use html control elements like checkboxes, sliders, or select boxes to trigger interactions. All interactions should be designed using visual elements of your charts.

You may create additional svgs to accommodate your different visualizations.

Hint: We didn't cover many kinds of interactions, yet. In the learning models, you can learn about click, mouse enter, mouse leave and custom events. You can apply your skills from the module to implement the interactions.

Please, describe and defend your visualization and interaction design in the "design_choices.md" (20%).
